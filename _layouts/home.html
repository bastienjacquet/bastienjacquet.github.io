About Me

In January 2016, I started the Computer Vision group at Kitware Europe. Kitware is a leader in the creation and support of open-source software and state of the art technology.
Our Computer Vision publishes opensource code under the KWIVER repository.
The reconstruction code I contributed to is Motion-imagery Aerial Photogrammetry Toolkit (MAP-TK).

Between 2011 and 2016, I have been a Ph.D. student in the Computer Vision & Geometry Group of the Institute of Visual Computing at the Swiss Federal Institute of Technology Zürich (ETH) under supervision of Prof. Marc Pollefeys.
I was also regularly visiting the Willow Group from Prof. Jean Ponce at INRIA Paris, France.

In 2010, I received two 'diplômes d’Ingénieur' (equivalent to MSc) from École Polytechnique and École des Ponts et Chaussées while obtaining in parallel a Master of Science in Computer Science with major Mathematics, Computer Vision and Machine Learning (MVA) from ENS Cachan.

Research interests

I am generally interested in enabling computers to understand the world through pictures or videos.
